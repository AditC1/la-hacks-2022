The Issue

  • 1960s - wealthy nations began exploring the location of nodules - valuable metals on the seabed..
    • UN aimed to ensure that every country, rich or poor, would have equitable access to these nodules.
      • Allocated specific areas of the seabed for developing nations.
    • In September of 2022, the New York Times exposed corporations for exploiting access to seabed nodule distribution data
      • The Metals Company - Australian firm caught bribing the UN for classified files

Our Solution

  • ElectriQuity utilizes the Ethereum blockchain network in order to create a secure, immutable queue to manage the distribution of nodules
  • Our hack aims to hold larger countries, corporations, and the UN accountable to the equitable distribution of nodules
  • Sets a precedent for the application of Web3 technologies in similar queue-based problems; proof of concept for a way to make global electrification an equitable process
